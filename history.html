HISTORY

ORIGINS

The first official mention of Golęczewo comes from 1313, although back then it was called Goleńcowo. For the next 600 years it changed hands frequently, only to be bought by the Prussian Colonisation Commission for 530,000 marks in 1901.

MODEL FOR NATION

Its aim was to build a model German village, which great reputation how to strengthen the power of the Empire in the annexed territories and eradicate the Polish spirit.

INFRASTRUCTURE

Enormous sums of money were invested in this huge undertaking, thanks to which in Golęczewo, renamed Golenhofen, stylish settlement houses, public buildings including a school, an inn, a milk catchment, a water pump and a laundry-bath complex were built within 4 years. The roads were paved and planted with even more greenery.

SETTLERS

The settlement houses located today on Dworcowa, Lipowa and Tysiąclecia Streets could only be bought by Germans of Evangelical faith who moved to Golenhofen from all over the Empire.

REPUTATION AND SPLENDOUR

Visitors flocked to the village in large numbers to proclaim its excellence and the mastery of Prussian thought in Germany, France or the United States. The idyll lasted until 1918, when, after Poland regained its independence, the German settlers sold their houses to the Poles and returned to the area that had been left to the Germans after the end of the First World War.

HISTORY ON THE STREETS

This unique period in the history of the village can be seen today during a pleasant walk along Dworcowa Street. Check out the houses along this street in the Monuments section.
