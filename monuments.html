MONUMENTS

Dworcowa Street has always been the main street of the village and many buildings built along it were representing the architectural styles used throughout the German Empire. On the cards you can read what characterises each building.

Section Under Construction

Address: Dworcowa 59

Former function: School and Evangelical church

Today: primary school and flats

In the original construction, a clock tower towered over the right-hand part of the church. The first school year was inaugurated as early as 1905. 39 pupils studied in a classroom above whose door was written: Be obedient to your teachers and listen to them!.

Address: Dworcowa 58

Former function: inn with accommodation

Today: grocery shop and flat

It was here that visitors and residents lived it up. The complex included a restaurant, bar, shop and playroom. Visitors would leave their steeds in the stable, right next to the owner's cows and pigs.

Address: Dworcowa 57

Function: residential house

The first inhabitant of the house was Johan Arwa with his family, who came from the area that today is around Belgrade in Serbia. It is definitely a prominent building in its height, forming together with the school and the inn the frame of the village centre.

Address: Dworcowa 35

Function: residential house

The unique colour of the shutters and some of the beams immediately draw the attention of the passer-by. The charming small windows, through which the incoming light illuminates the staircase, create an interesting asymmetry on the front wall.

Address: Dworcowa 29/31

Function: residential house

Today a semi-detached house, but formerly an apartment building combined with a barn - a controversial combination.

Address: Dworcowa 20

Function: Train station

Renovated in 2020, the building remains unchanged as it was built in 1906. Inside the waiting room there are documents from the last century that show the non-standard, from our perspective, railway deliveries to Golenhofen.
